Artist Statement

My creative foundation lies in the formative influence of classical Chinese literature on my inner world during adolescence, and in my ongoing, self-directed exploration of the deep connections between the human spirit and the intricate order of nature. This follows the traditional artistic maxim “wai shi zao hua, zhong de xin yuan （外师造化，中得心源）”—learning from nature as an external teacher, while drawing inspiration from the wellspring of one’s own heart.

Through a deep engagement with, and personal deconstruction of, the most traditional brush-and-ink systems of Chinese painting, I work with multi-layered washes and improvisational brushstrokes rooted in calligraphy. In my practice, the tonal depth of ink engages in dialogue with the linear structure of calligraphy, opening a space for the emergence of a new order.

My work reconnects the mastery of traditional brushwork and classical landscape interpretation with the realities of contemporary life, translating the calligraphic brush spirit of the East into the dynamic force of Western Abstract Expressionism. Some pieces offer humorous or reflective commentary on the state of daily life—an approach born from my immersive engagement with the rhythms and textures of urban street culture.

I emphasize immediacy and intuition in my process, allowing color and line to grow, collide, and merge spontaneously on xuan paper. This pursuit of an “emergent” state, beyond predetermined plans, leads me to advocate for “letting the work grow on its own.”

I continue to explore the limits of Chinese ink as a pure carrier of emotion, as well as the shifts in spatial perception brought by changes in material and scale. My aim is to create artistic practices that, within spiritually attuned contemporary exhibition spaces, embody both powerful visual tension and profound meditative depth.
